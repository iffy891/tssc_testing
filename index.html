About the TSNZ and the Sport in New Zealand

TSNZ information:

Indoor target shooting is the heart of TSNZ, with over 1,000 members shooting regularly at indoor ranges across New Zealand. It is welcoming, affordable, and suitable for beginners of all ages, with many clubs offering equipment and support to help new shooters get started.

The Indoor Season runs from 1 April to 30 September, with clubs typically shooting weekly and a wide range of competitions available throughout the season.

The Sport in New Zealand:

Indoor Smallbore shooting uses .22 calibre rifles over distances of 20 or 25 yards and is shot from the prone position, meaning shooters lie on the ground.

Beginners start by resting the front of the rifle on a stand to make learning easier. As skills develop, shooters progress to wearing a shooting jacket and sling and supporting the rifle themselves. Coaching and guidance are usually available at club nights to help new shooters build confidence and good technique.

Most indoor ranges in New Zealand are 25 yards, with some 20-yard ranges, particularly in country areas of the South Island. Target sizes vary slightly to ensure scores are comparable across all ranges.

Targets and Matches:

Most indoor ranges use 11-bull paper targets:

The centre bull is used for three sighting shots, which are not scored

One scoring shot is fired at each of the remaining ten bulls

Each shot can score a maximum of 10.1, giving a perfect score of 100.10

Matches are typically:

10 shots in 12 minutes, or
20 shots in 22 minutes

One indoor range in New Zealand currently uses electronic targets. These have a single aiming mark, with scores displayed instantly for both shooters and spectators in the social area.

Indoor Benchrest Shooting:

Indoor Benchrest shooting is a newer discipline within TSNZ and is currently offered as a separate event at a small number of clubs.

Benchrest shooting is especially appealing to beginners or those who prefer a seated shooting position. Shooters use standard indoor rifles and sights, but instead of holding the rifle, it is supported on front and rear rests, and the shooter sits at a bench rather than lying prone.

TSNZ currently has Guidelines in place for Indoor Benchrest shooting. As participation grows, these will continue to be developed into formal Rules.

Grading and Progression

Shooters are graded at the end of each Indoor Season based on their performance. Grades ensure fair competition and allow shooters to compete against others of similar ability.

Grades are:

D Grade: up to 89.99
C Grade: 90.00 – 93.99
B Grade: 94.00 – 96.49
A Grade: 96.50 – 97.74
Master: 97.75 – 100.00

New shooters usually begin in D Grade and may move up or down each season depending on results.
